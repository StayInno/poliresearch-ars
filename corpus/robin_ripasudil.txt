Robin: a multi-agent system for automating scientific discovery (Ghareeb et al., 2025; later in Nature).

Robin orchestrates three FutureHouse agents in iterative hypothesis -> experiment -> analysis
cycles. Crow performs broad literature search and question answering. Falcon specialises in
evaluating and ranking candidate molecules against the literature. Finch is the data-analysis
agent: it runs code on experimental data (including RNA-seq), finds patterns, and produces the
manuscript figures. Humans execute only the physical bench experiments.

Discovery. Targeting dry age-related macular degeneration (dAMD), Robin hypothesised that
enhancing retinal pigment epithelium (RPE) phagocytosis could be therapeutic. A first round found
that the ROCK inhibitor Y-27632 increased RPE phagocytosis; Finch's RNA-seq analysis showed
Y-27632 upregulates ABCA1, a lipid-efflux transporter, proposing the mechanism. A second round
proposed new ROCK-inhibitor candidates, with the top hit being ripasudil, a drug already approved
for glaucoma, repositioned here for dAMD. Reported effect: an increase in RPE phagocytosis
relative to vehicle control.

Validation strength. End-to-end in roughly 2.5 months. All hypotheses, experiment choices, and
figures were AI-generated; the wet-lab work was human-run. Results are in vitro (cell culture),
not clinical.
